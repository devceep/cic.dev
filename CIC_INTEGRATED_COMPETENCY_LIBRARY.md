## 1. Introduction and Framework Overview

### 1.1 Purpose

This Integrated Competency Library establishes the official competency framework for the Credit Information Corporation (CIC), the Philippines' central credit registry mandated under **Republic Act No. 9510** (Credit Information System Act). This library serves as the foundation for:

- Performance management and appraisal
- Recruitment and selection
- Promotion and succession planning
- Learning and development planning
- Career pathing and job profiling
- Audit, CSC, DBM, and Board compliance review

### 1.2 Legal and Regulatory Framework

This competency library is aligned with:

| Reference | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **RA 9510 (CISA)** | Credit Information System Act - CIC's enabling law |
| **RA 10173 (DPA)** | Data Privacy Act of 2012 |
| **EO 95** | Executive Order on Ease of Doing Business |
| **CSC MC No. 05, s. 2016** | Leadership Competency Framework for the Philippine Public Sector |
| **Generic Competency Dictionary** | Philippine Public Sector Generic Competency Dictionary |
| **CSC PRIME-HRM** | Program to Institutionalize Meritocracy and Excellence in HR Management |

### 1.4 CRISPIEE Core Values

The CIC Core Competencies are anchored on the CRISPIEE values:

| Letter | Value | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **C** | Customer Centricity | Prioritizing stakeholder needs and delivering responsive public service |
| **R** | Results-Driven | Focusing on measurable outcomes and organizational goals |
| **I** | Integrity | Upholding ethical standards and public accountability |
| **S** | Solution-Oriented | Proactively addressing challenges with practical approaches |
| **P** | Precision | Ensuring accuracy and attention to detail in all undertakings |
| **I** | Innovation | Embracing continuous improvement and creative solutions |
| **E** | Excellence | Striving for the highest standards in public service |

For Leadership and Functional Competencies, four proficiency levels are defined:

| Level | Name | Description | Typical Application |
|-------|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **1** | Basic | Demonstrates foundational knowledge and skills; applies competency under supervision; follows established procedures | Entry-level, Rank-and-File |
| **2** | Intermediate | Applies competency independently in routine situations; guides others in basic applications; recognizes when to escalate | Experienced Staff, Supervisors |
| **3** | Advanced | Applies competency in complex situations; leads initiatives; develops solutions for non-routine challenges; mentors others | Managers, Department Heads |
| **4** | Superior | Strategic application; shapes organizational direction; recognized expert; influences sector-wide practices | Executives, Senior Leaders |

# PART I: CORE COMPETENCIES

Core Competencies reflect the fundamental values and behaviors expected of **ALL CIC employees**, regardless of position or rank. These competencies embody the CRISPIEE core values and are assessed through behavioral indicators rather than proficiency levels.

---

## C1: Integrity and Public Accountability

### Definition

Demonstrates unwavering commitment to ethical conduct, transparency, and honesty in all official actions and decisions. Upholds the public trust inherent in government service by ensuring that personal actions reflect the highest standards of probity. Takes responsibility for decisions and actions, and is accountable for results to the public, oversight bodies, and the organization.

### CRISPIEE Alignment
- **Integrity** (Primary)
- **Equitable** (Supporting)

### Behavioral Indicators

| # | Behavioral Indicator |
|---|---------------------|
| 1 | Acts with honesty and transparency in all transactions, ensuring that official actions are free from personal bias, favoritism, or conflict of interest |
| 2 | Adheres to the Code of Conduct and Ethical Standards for Public Officials and Employees (RA 6713) and complies with all applicable laws, regulations, and CIC policies |
| 3 | Accepts responsibility for mistakes, promptly discloses errors, and takes corrective action without attempting to conceal or shift blame |
| 4 | Safeguards confidential information and public resources, and reports any irregularities, misconduct, or violations of ethical standards through appropriate channels |

---

### CRISPIEE Alignment

| 3 | Ensures that all work outputs meet or exceed established quality standards, are thoroughly reviewed for accuracy, and are delivered within committed timelines |
| 4 | Actively seeks and acts on stakeholder feedback to continuously improve service processes, reduce inefficiencies, and enhance stakeholder satisfaction |

# PART III: FUNCTIONAL COMPETENCIES

Functional Competencies are **role-specific technical competencies** that reflect the specialized knowledge and skills required for CIC's mandate as the national credit information registry. These competencies are assessed across four proficiency levels and are mapped to specific organizational units based on primary role requirements.

---

## F1: Data Privacy and Protection

### Definition

Ensures compliance with the **Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173)**, National Privacy Commission (NPC) issuances, and related regulations in the collection, processing, storage, and dissemination of credit information. Implements appropriate organizational, physical, and technical security measures to protect personal and sensitive personal information. Upholds the fundamental right of data subjects to privacy while enabling CIC to fulfill its mandate as custodian of 66+ million credit records.

### Regulatory Framework
- Republic Act No. 10173 (Data Privacy Act of 2012)
- NPC Circular 16-01 (Security of Personal Data)
- NPC Circular 16-03 (Breach Notification)
- CIC Data Privacy Manual

---

### Level 1: Basic

**Description:** Understands basic data privacy principles, follows data handling procedures, identifies personal information, and reports potential breaches.

| # | Behavioral Indicator |
|---|---------------------|

| 2 | Follows established data handling procedures for collection, processing, storage, and transmission of credit information and personal data |
| 3 | Correctly identifies and classifies personal information, sensitive personal information, and privileged information in the course of work |
| 4 | Recognizes and immediately reports potential privacy incidents, security breaches, or unauthorized access through appropriate channels |

---

### Level 2: Intermediate

| # | Behavioral Indicator |

| 4 | Processes data subject access requests, correction requests, and erasure requests in accordance with NPC guidelines and CIC procedures |

---

### Level 3: Advanced

**Description:** Designs privacy-by-design systems, leads compliance initiatives, manages complex breach incidents, and advises on regulatory interpretations.

| # | Behavioral Indicator |
|---|---------------------|

| 2 | Leads organization-wide data privacy compliance initiatives, ensuring CIC meets all NPC registration, reporting, and compliance requirements |

| 4 | Provides authoritative guidance on interpretation of data privacy regulations and their application to CIC operations and initiatives |

---

### Level 4: Superior

| # | Behavioral Indicator |

## F2: Credit Information Management

### Definition

Manages the complete lifecycle of credit information—from collection and validation through consolidation, storage, and dissemination—in accordance with **Republic Act No. 9510 (Credit Information System Act)** and implementing rules. Ensures the accuracy, completeness, timeliness, and integrity of data received from Submitting Entities and provided to Accessing Entities. Applies expertise in credit bureau operations, data quality management, and regulatory compliance to support CIC's mandate as the Philippines' central credit registry serving 66+ million data subjects.

### Regulatory Framework
- Republic Act No. 9510 (Credit Information System Act)
- Implementing Rules and Regulations of RA 9510
- Executive Order No. 95 (Ease of Doing Business)
- CIC Circular Letters and Guidelines

---

### Level 1: Basic

| # | Behavioral Indicator |
|---|---------------------|
| 1 | Demonstrates understanding of the credit information lifecycle from collection through dissemination as defined under RA 9510 |
| 2 | Performs data entry, encoding, and initial validation tasks accurately and efficiently using established systems and tools |
| 3 | Follows data quality control procedures, including validation checks, error identification, and escalation of data issues |
| 4 | Operates credit information systems, databases, and applications correctly and in accordance with established protocols |

---

### Level 2: Intermediate

**Description:** Manages credit data quality processes, coordinates with Submitting Entities, resolves data discrepancies, and produces standard credit reports.

| # | Behavioral Indicator |

## F3: Financial Analysis and Assessment

### Definition

Applies financial analysis skills to assess and verify Submitting Entities, evaluate organizational financial health, support budget planning, and ensure responsible stewardship of public resources. Evaluates financial compliance, operational capacity, and risk profiles of regulated institutions. Supports evidence-based decision-making through rigorous financial assessment aligned with DBM, COA, and BSP standards applicable to a GOCC with regulatory functions.

### Regulatory Framework
- Department of Budget and Management Guidelines
- Commission on Audit Rules and Regulations
- Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas Prudential Standards (for SE assessment)
- RA 9510 Submitting Entity Requirements

---

### Level 1: Basic

**Description:** Understands financial statements, calculates standard ratios, prepares routine reports, and follows financial analysis procedures.

| # | Behavioral Indicator |

| 3 | Prepares routine financial reports, schedules, and supporting documents accurately and within prescribed timelines |
| 4 | Follows established financial analysis procedures, methodologies, and templates in completing assigned analytical tasks |

| # | Behavioral Indicator |
